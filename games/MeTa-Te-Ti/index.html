⊞ How to Play

The Setup

The board is a 3×3 grid of small tic-tac-toe boards (9 boards total), laid out in a larger 3×3 grid. Your goal: win 3 small boards in a row on that big grid — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

When you play in cell 5 of any small board, your opponent must play next inside small board 5 (the center board). The cell you pick always sends your opponent to the matching board.

Step-by-Step Example

  • ✕ plays first — free to pick any board. They click cell 3 of board 1 → ✕ goes in top-right of board 1.
  • ○ must now play in board 3 (top-right board). They click cell 7 → board 3, bottom-left cell.
  • ✕ must now play in board 7 (bottom-left board). And so on.

Winning a Small Board

Get 3 of your marks in a row inside any small board (row, column, or diagonal) to claim that board. It becomes yours and is marked with a big ✕ or ○. Filled boards that nobody won are draws.

Free Move

If the cell you played would send your opponent to a board that is already finished (won or drawn), the opponent gets a free move — they can play in any open board they like. Look for the pulsing gold outline to know when you have a free move.

Winning the Game

The first player to claim 3 small boards in a row on the big grid wins MeTa-Te-Ti. Think two moves ahead — every cell you play is also a board you're sending your opponent to!
